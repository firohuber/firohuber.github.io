Introduction & Leadership For Others Takeaways

Over this topic, we covered the difference between being a leader and a manager.
During the class, we discussed interesting phenomena in human interaction and defined what leadership is.

Phenomena in human interaction

  • Escalation of commitment - the further you progress, it is harder to give up
  • As social beings, we tend to overvalue people and undervalue the situation
  • At high levels, people who tend to defer to others were promoted more often
  • When things go well, we attribute it to ourselves, but when things donâ€™t go well, we defer and attribute it to others or the situation

What is leadership

  • Leaders set direction, and it is harder to evaluate or value
  • Leadership is not a position but a set of behaviors
  • Leading is promoting change, and people don't like change. Therefore, if you're leading, you're upsetting people
  • Leadership has become more important because the world we live in changes much faster than it used to
